Chapter 2

Characters referenced in this chapter: Bitch, Emma, Endbringers, Regent/Alec, Shadow Stalker/Sophia, Skitter/Taylor, Slaughterhouse Nine, Tattletale/Lisa

Traveling alone to see Tattletale.
Set a Course: (+4 supply) [7, 2 vs 5] Weak hit. Face a complication at the destination.
Story Complication: [Unexpected enemies appear] Make sense. But who?
Campaign Elements: [Do the ends justify the means?] Shadow Stalker. She wants revenge for what Regent did to her. I'm going to change the canon to say that she opted to run instead of getting arrested.

Just a few blocks before reaching Lisa's place, Shadow Stalker steps into the street to block me.

"Skitter," she says, like it left a bad taste in her mouth.

"Shadow Stalker? I thought you left town."

She laughs bitterly. "No teams anywhere would have me. And if I'm going to go solo, I'd rather do it in my home town." She raises her crossbow at me. I can see that she is no longer using the tranquilizer arrows. "I just need to clean it up first."

"You're kidding me. You're practically a villain yourself," I say and begin gathering my bugs.

Secure an Advantage: (+3 wits) [5, 7 vs 8] Strong hit. Take +2 momentum (3) and take +1 forward. There's a good amount of bugs in the area in addition to what I've brought with me.

Enter the Fray: (+2 heart, +1 forward) [4, 9 vs 4] Miss. In a bad spot. Add Fend Off Shadow Stalker (formidable) as combat objective.

"Don't compare us!" she growls and fires her crossbow. I'm slow to react, but I move to dodge it.

React Under Fire: (+2 edge) [2, 5 vs 4] Weak hit. Suffer -1 health (3). She grazes my arm.
Endure Harm: (+3 health) [3, 8 vs 5] Weak hit. Suffer -1 momentum (2) and take +1 health (4). The armor takes it but staggers me.

The arrow glances off the armor and makes me take a step back. I quickly head for the cover of a nearby alleyway and Shadow Stalker gives pursuit. Unlike me, she isn't restricted to the narrow passage and can travel through the walls.

React Under Fire: (+2 edge) [1, 1 vs 8] Strong hit with a match. Take +1 momentum and in control.

Using a bug barrier to block her view of me, I manage to break her line of sight and I turn right while she turns left. She realizes a few seconds later that she made a mistake, but I've already put a good distance between us.

I quickly duck into a doorway and wait. After a few moments Shadow Stalker passes in front of me. I attempt to punch her in the kidney from behind.

Strike: (+1 iron) [9, 1 vs 4] Weak hit. Mark 2 progress (2) but in a bad spot.

Shadow Stalker lets out a short scream from the blow to her side, then immediately turns into mist and passes through me. I feel her arms wrap around me and she holds the tip of her arrow to my neck. I don't think it'd be enough to pass through the spider-silk of my costume, but it's not an ideal situation regardless.

"Don't you fucking move, bug girl," she growls.

I follow her instruction. However, she said nothing of my bugs. I send a swarm to attack her.

React Under Fire: (+3 wits) [1, 2 vs 7] Strong hit. In control. I manage free myself.

She doesn't have a lot of skin exposed but it barely matters. The bugs crawl into the small gaps in her clothing, biting at her sensitive bits. She turns into mist to avoid them which leaves me unrestrained. I spin around and spray the pepper spray in her general direction.

Strike: (+2 edge) [4, 3 vs 6] Strong hit. Mark progress twice (4).

Shadow Stalker scatters, possibly in panic but it's difficult to tell while she's in mist form. I follow her with bursts of spray to ward her off. Doing so kills a few of my bugs, but it's worth it.

I pull out my lighter. "If I lit this cloud while it was mixed with your mist, do you think it'd be as effective as electricity?"

This is a taunt to gain leverage.
Gain Ground: (+3 wits) [6, 2 vs 9] Strong hit. Mark progress (5) and take +2 momentum (4). I definitely scare her off a bit.

The mist quickly pulls away from the area around me and reappears further down the alley. Shadow Stalker looks a bit ragged. I form multiple bug clones and talk through them.

"Why are you here, Shadow Stalker?" I ask.

Action+Theme: [Oppose Phenomenon] She's opposing our takeover.

"Your shitty group managed to take over the city while I was gone. I'm here to fix that," she says.

I laugh. "You're an idiot. We're the only thing holding this city together. The heroes aren't doing shit to help, and they're even letting the Nine run wild. We're the only ones trying to save this shithole."

I'm trying to make her reconsider.
Gain Ground: (+2 heart) [4, 6 vs 6] Weak hit. Mark progress (6). She agrees about the heroes but doesn't think it applies to her.

"That's on them. I'm no hero; I'm a fucking vigilante. While the heroes are bogged down in the bureaucratic bullshit, I'll be taking back the city."

"You're going to stop the Nine singlehandedly then?" I ask, my voice full of doubt. When she doesn't respond, I continue, "You know, I'm surprised that the Nine didn't ask you to join. I thought for sure that you'd be a candidate."

I am trying to make her doubt her stance.
Gain Ground: (+3 wits) [6, 8 vs 6] Miss. In a bad spot. She turns it back on me.

Shadow Stalker shakes her head. "I don't do groups and I don't work with villains. I hurt villains. Villains who hurt innocents. Villains like you, bug girl." She raises her crossbow.

Shadow Stalker attacks and I attempt to dodge.
React Under Fire: (+2 edge, 4 armor) [5, 3 vs 6] Strong hit. Take 1 momentum (5) and in control. The armor deflects the arrow and I hide from Shadow Stalker.

I bolt toward the alley just as the arrow fires. It glances off the armor on my back. I enter the alley and continue running.

As I near the end of the alley, I disburse my swarm into multiple clones and position them in different locations: one on the roof, one peering around the corner of the alley entrance, one tucked into a doorway. As for myself, I loop around and enter another alley that leads to Shadow Stalker, hoping to flank her or, even better, attack her from behind.

I attempt to track Shadow Stalker.
Gain Ground: (+3 wits) [9, 9 vs 6] Miss with a match. In a bad spot. She predicted my movement.

I duck into the shadows of a ruined building and attempt to locate Shadow Stalker with my bugs. Unfortunately, what I discover is her mist only seconds before it enters through the wall behind me. She materializes in a second and kicks me in the spine, sending me sprawling on the ground.

I use my swarm to put distance between us.
React Under Fire: (+2 edge, +2 bug health) [9, 3 vs 6] Weak hit. Suffer -1 spirit (4). I get away but she rattles me a bit.
Endure Stress: (+4 spirit) [7, 6 vs 6] Miss. Suffer -1 spirit (3). It hits me even harder.

I put up a wall of bugs to block her view of me as I try to scramble away.

Shadow Stalker waves the bugs away while trying to protect her face. She turns to mist and re-enters through the wall, then appears further down the alley, clear of my swarm.

"God, you're so fucking annoying. You remind me of another annoying bitch that I'm forced to deal with on a daily basis because she also refuses to die," she growls. "In fact… If I look closely, you look a lot like her. Same hair, same build."

My heart practically stops. Did she figure out my identity? What would she do if she knew? I would say that she'd probably kill me, but I'm pretty sure that's already her plan.

If she's scrutinizing me that closely, then I should probably distract her. I bring the swarms that I had stationed around the alleyways and position them behind her. Through them I say, "You're nothing but a bully, Shadow Stalker."

I'm trying to catch her off guard.
React Under Fire: (+1 shadow, +2 bug health) [2, 1 vs 9] Strong hit. In control and take +1 momentum (6). I startle her.

Shadow Stalker spins around to face my clones. My swarm bodies dissolve into thousands of bugs as they blanket her. She lets out an uncharacteristic shriek and falls backward while desperately trying to get them off of her.

I book it out of there before she composes herself enough to remember that she can just mist right through them. As I round the corner, I attempt to form decoys just like before.

Gain Ground: (+3 wits, +2 bug health) [5, 6 vs 6] Weak hit. Mark progress (7). I form a trio of decoys.

What is Shadow Stalker doing now?
Combat Action: [Hide or sneak] She mists, as expected.

I can feel Shadow Stalker mist through the swarm I left behind, though it gives her apparent difficulty. Once free, she floats after me at high speed. I'll never outrun her at this pace, so I disguise my location with my decoys. The three of them run parallel with me. As Shadow Stalker enters the same alleyway as me, my swarms and I split up.

Gain Ground: (+3 wits, +2 bug health) [8, 9 vs 8] Miss. In a bad spot. She's not fooled.

Possibly out of pure luck, Shadow Stalker picks me out of the group. I feel her level her crossbow at me as I sprint down the alley, and I immediately send one of my swarms at her. She fires at my back as they descend upon her.

Clash: (+2 edge, +2 bug health) [1, 3 vs 6] Strong hit. Mark progress twice (9), take +1 momentum (7), and in control. She misses.

Shadow Stalker is taken by surprise and the shot goes wide. She screams and collapses to the ground as the bugs tear into her, biting and stinging.

I stand over her as she writhes under my swarm. "Stay down, Shadow Stalker."

Take Decisive Action: (9 progress) [3, 10] Weak hit. Remove Fend Off Shadow Stalker combat objective. [You face collateral damage: Something is lost, damaged, or broken] She identifies me.

I hear a choking laugh come from her. "That voice. You've got to be fucking shitting me." She crawls to her knees before she mists through my swarm and reforms a few feet away.

She's not moving or attacking, just glaring at me. I tilt my head questioningly in response.

"Taylor," she announces, confident in her assessment.

My body stiffens at that single word. Unsure how to proceed, I withdraw my bugs and gather them around me.

"I know it's you. I'd recognize that whiney loser voice anywhere." She stalks toward me. "No wonder you always talk to me through your creepy little bugs, you freak."

My bugs are practically vibrating around me. I have to put effort into keeping them at bay. Meanwhile Shadow Stalker, or perhaps I should say Sophia, is circling around me. Assessing me.

"It all fits," she mutters. "This is why you took me hostage after the Endbringer attack. Fucking bitch, you ruined my life."

"Regent ruined your life. I had no say in that." I cross my arms. "Besides, it was your own ammunition that violated your parole."

Shadow Stalker waves her hand in dismissal. "They wanted me to use tranq darts on rapists. Fuck that."

I shrug. "That's the price for being a hero."

She scoffs and continues to circle me. "I'm no hero. I just like taking out villains." Then she stops in front of me and looks into my eyes. "But maybe in order to defeat the monster, you have to become one yourself. Isn't that what you did? I heard that you were just a hero faking to be a villain. Little Taylor, the traitor."

I glare at her, though I know she can't see it through my mask.

Shadow Stalker laughs. "What a fucking joke! As a villain you've done more in the last three months than the heroes have accomplished in years."

"Fucking bureaucratic bullshit," I grumble and cross my arms.

"Fucking bureaucratic bullshit," she agrees.

It seems like Sophia is trying to connect with me.
Make a Connection: (+2 heart) [4, 6 vs 5] Weak hit. Add Shadow Stalker (formidable) as connection. She demands to come with me.

After posturing at each other for a moment, I sigh and turn away from Shadow Stalker. "This has been fun, but I have things to do," I say and start down the alley and toward the connecting street. However, my bugs inform me that Shadow Stalker is trailing behind me.

"Where are we going?" she asks.

"Huh?!" I stop and spin to face her.

She crosses her arms and smirks at me. God, I hate her. Standing there like that, I can practically see Emma standing beside her, the two of them laughing at my expense.

"We're not going anywhere. I am going to meet a friend." I turn and start walking again. Sophia follows.

"Silly Taylor. You don't have any friends," she taunts me as she trails in my wake. When I don't respond, she laughs. "I love that you haven't even attempted to deny your identity. I have to admit, I wasn't entirely sure that I was right, but you've all but confirmed it."

"Fine. You got me, Sophia. Happy?" I growl at her.

She's quiet for a moment. "… Yes. Actually." There's an odd quality to her voice, like she's surprised at her own answer. "I thought that you were the prey, but you've been the predator all along. Good for you." That sounded like honest praise. It surprises me enough that I take a look a her, and from her body language she looks very pleased with herself. Is she…taking credit for my success? Fucking Sophia.

"Why are you stalking me, Sophia?" I ask, exasperated.

"Playing hero didn't work. And thanks to you, I'm not even a vigilante anymore, I'm a straight up villain."

"And?" I prod her to get to the point.

"And you've taken over the fucking city. Not just taken it over, but taken out the villains as well." She waits, apparently for me to come to a conclusion on my own but I'm just not following her logic, so she continues, "I'm not much of a team player, as you know, but I'll be happy as long as I can shoot a few villains."

I stop and turn to face her again. I'm livid. "Are you fucking kidding me, Sophia? You're requesting to join my team?"

She smiles at me. "You break it, you buy it. It only seems fair that I join the team responsible for ruining my career as both a hero and a vigilante." When that flimsy excuse doesn't win me over, she follows with "I won't follow prey. I can only exist beside predators like myself. You and your team are predators. Top of the food chain."

"The Nine are further up the food chain," I counter. But then I wonder: am I so bitter that I'd rather fight Shadow Stalker as a member of the Nine than have her as a teammate?

She just shakes her head. "Not that they asked me, but I wouldn't join them. Even I have limits. I won't work with anyone that are so thoroughly evil. Those are my targets."

I tilt my head. I wonder how she feels about Alec then. That aside, her logic is fairly sound, and we could definitely use more firepower against the Nine. I also wonder what would happen to Sophia if I said no. Would she be killed by the Nine? Or would they recruit her when realize they that Bitch won't accept their offer? Or would we eventually have to take her out for good ourselves? If I can get over my bias, accepting her as a teammate may be the lesser evil.

"You're so creepy," Sophia says suddenly.

"Hmm?" I ask. Then I realize that my bugs have been swarming over my body, as well as traveling across my mask and lenses. The effect is probably disturbing. I didn't even notice that it was happening since I rarely rely on my eyes to see lately.

I wave a hand to dismiss her, and the bugs disappear into the spaces in my costume designed to house them. "Whatever. I'll have to run this by my team." I sigh. "I guess you can come with. I was headed to see Tattletale anyway."
