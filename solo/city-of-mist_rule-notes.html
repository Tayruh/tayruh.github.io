City of Mist - Rule Notes


Story Tags

Story tags are used to describe two categories of story elements: 1) temporary abilities, qualities, perks, and items possessed by your character (eg. "wolf-form" or "cleaver"), and 2) independent objects, beings, concepts, or effects included in a scene (eg. "the police" or "pouring rain").

Tags never represent the current state of a person or object (eg. "mortally wounded" or "dead"), unless they represent the essential nature of someone.

Temporary story tags expire after a single invocation and cannot be burnt for a hit. Ongoing story tags last until they narratively expire or until they are burnt. Permanent story tags do not expire (and thus can be burnt and recovered).

The MC can create story tags whenever they want, including giving the player a temporary story tag for a move (such as "leverage" for Convince) if it was already existing in the narrative without requiring CtG.

Power Tags

Power tags can only be replaced during a moment of evolution (which is only once per character), or when you replace a theme, so be sure that any tags you give your PC are intrinsic to them.

It's best to keep the average amount of a new PC's power tags used for a move to only 1 or 2, which results in a 72% to 83% chance of a hit. Alternatively, you can use grit mode which scales down the power.

Power tags belonging to crew and extras are crispy, meaning that they are burnt once used. Crew tags can be recovered using GODC and extra tags recover with Montage.

Statuses

Statuses reflect temporary conditions of all kinds that affect your character, such as physical conditions, psychological conditions, social conditions, supernatural or mystical conditions, and combat situations. (eg. injuries, cheerfulness, humiliated, cursed, or shielded)

Statuses always represent transient states. They never represent things (eg. "guns-2"), or permanent qualities (eg. "smart-5"). For things or permanent qualities use tags.

The MC can hit the player with a status whenever they want, but the player can usually FD against it.

When attempting to a remove a status, the status usually doesn't count against the move to reduce it. However the GM may decide that a status is especially difficult to remove and declare that it does count against its removal.

The playable tiers are tier-1 (minor), tier-2 (mild), tier-3 (major), and tier-4 (dramatic). You can still act while these are relevant to your action but they will affect the power.

Tier-5 (OUT) is the tipping point in which a negative status shuts down your ability to take actions that go against it (eg. "encased-in-a-web-5"), a beneficial status renders you incapable of performing actions related to that status (eg. "focused-3" becomes "tunnel-visioned-5"), and in both cases the status no longer provides a bonus to anyone's power.

Tier-6 (MC) status means the PC is dead, close to it, or taken over by the MC. The max tier is always 6, and any tier beyond that is reduced to 6.

While you may reduce tiers 1 through 5 with game actions, tier-6 is permanent and unable to be reduced through normal means. When appropriate and with the player’s approval, the MC may choose to trade the tier-6 status for the loss of any number of power tags, the addition or loss of weakness tags, and/or the replacement of an entire theme. This can represent the permanent transformation the character has undergone. The character’s tier-6 status is then reduced to tier 5.

When attempting to a remove a status, the status usually doesn't count against the move to reduce it. However the GM may decide that a status is especially difficult to remove and declare that it does count against its removal.

Status Stacking and Reducing

A higher-tier status replaces a lower-tier status in the same spectrum. When you stack a lower-tier status with a higher-tier status, the remaining ticks after increasing to the next tier carry over. (eg. 3.2 + 2 = 4.1)

Temporary statuses do not stack with prolonged statuses.

The ticks required to increase a status to the next tier is equal to its current tier. (eg. from 2 to 3 is 2 ticks, from 3 to 4 is 3 ticks, etc)

When a status is reduced, all pips are cleared. (eg. 4.3 - 2 = 2) When a move reduces a created status, its tier is reduced before being applied to the target.

Juice

Juice is primarily created using CtG, but can also be gained by other moves like HWAYG. Juice has a method, which is the action taken to create the juice. Juice can be spent at any time to pick items off the CtG menu as long as the method is still narratively relevant.

Batches of juice exist until they narratively expire, which is usually after just a moment. You may have multiple batches but they do not stack.

While a batch exists, any tags or statuses used to create that batch cannot be used in a CtG move, and you cannot CtG using the same or similar method.

Any tag or status created with juice is temporary by default. Tags and statuses created using help and hurt points are also temporary.

Tags created by juice must be distinct. You also cannot spend juice to increase tiers of an existing status, but you may create a status on the same spectrum and stack them.

Themes

A nascent theme is a newly added theme that begins with only one power tag. When you first mark attention on a nascent theme, it gains another power tag. When you first complete the attention track on a nascent theme, it gains another power tag, making it a standard theme.

When the crew has a new overarching goal, it gets a new nascent crew theme. When the crew has achieved its goal, it gets a new nascent extra theme.

Linked Moves

With the exception of FD, you cannot repeat an action using the same tags, until the conditions have changed.

Each tag can only be used once within a set of linked moves, even if the tag is available and relevant to more than one move in the set. (eg. if you use a tag in HWAYG and miss, you cannot use the tag with FD to avoid the incoming status, since HWAYG leads directly into FD.)

Moves that have the same purpose are also considered linked moves, even if they don’t follow one another. (eg. you cannot use a "spell book" tag with CtG to create a "fireball" tag or "magical-barrier-1" status and then use "spell book" in any move in which "fireball" or "magical-barrier-1" is used, since the purpose of using the spell book was to cast a fireball or shield from magic.)

Player Moves

Go Toe to Toe is the basic opposed roll move. You use it when someone is attempting to do something to you that you don't want, or you're attempting to do something to them that they don't want, and both of you are in a position to fight for what you want.

Hit With All You've Got is like GTtT except you get a free shot, guaranteeing damage on a hit and allowing you to scale it up in area or intensity. The enemy can still react after though.

Take the Risk is when you're attempting to do something dangerous that isn't opposed.

Face Danger is only used in reaction to already receiving a status, whether from an MC hard move or another player. The move is the reflexive attempt to endure or avoid the incoming status. If you're reacting to a soft move rather than a hard move (ie. you still have a chance to avoid or block the harm rather than endure), use GTtT or TtR instead.

Change the Game

Where GTtT and HWAYG are about giving a status to opposition directly using applicable tags, CtG creates juice which can then be spent to create an advantage by repurposing tags as new story tags or statuses (eg. turning "spell book" into "fireball" or "magical-barrier-1") or removing tags and statuses.

If the player is trying to inflict a status and is opposed, they should use GTtT instead. If the player is trying to inflict a status and they are unopposed, they may instead use HWAYG, which provides a much better outcome (eg. ongoing by default with chance to increase tier) but with higher risk (eg. collateral damage and retaliation on hit).

Stop. Holding. Back.

A "significant" sacrifice befits a significant change: changing a detail that will affect the course of a case/session, permanently defeating a minor danger, temporarily evading or surviving the main danger of a case/session, overcoming or circumventing a significant challenge or circumstance, effecting a change with small repercussions on the entire series, or anything that is not covered by the character’s power tags but is on the same general scale as them.

A "no-return" sacrifice befits a change from which there is no return: changing a detail that will affect the course of a season, permanently defeating a major danger, resolving one of the main challenges of a case/session, temporarily evading or surviving the main danger of a season, overcoming or circumventing a major challenge or circumstance, or effecting a change with major repercussions on the entire series.

The "ultimate" sacrifice befits the ultimate change: changing a detail that will affect the course of the entire series, permanently defeating a top-tier danger, resolving one of the main challenges of a season, temporarily evading or surviving the main danger of a series, overcoming or circumventing an extreme challenge or circumstance, or effecting a change with dramatic repercussions on the entire series.

Danger Statuses

When giving a status to a danger, you must choose one of the following applications: 1) the status counts, against that danger’s spectrums to promote its defeat, 2) the status applies to player moves against that danger, or 3) the status applies to the danger’s own moves.

Option 1 functions as a health track, so you cannot use it to affect your moves or a danger's moves. Option 2 affects the player's power when applicable, but does not affect the statuses that dangers give or receive. Option 3 increases or decreases the statuses that dangers give or receive using standard stacking and reducing rules for statuses, but does not affect player's power.

While the MC may decide to give a status multiple applications, be aware that doing so may decrease the challenge drastically. (eg. if you pick both option 1 and 2, that status becomes a death spiral.)

Danger Spectrums

Any status given to a danger that is on their spectrum cannot be added to your roll against them.

A danger is not defeated until a spectrum hits its max tier. Spectrums can only be maxed out with ongoing statuses.

You may give them off-spectrum statuses (ie. statuses not given a spectrum in their danger profile) that function like a typical status, which aren't required to promote the danger's defeat and may be applied to the player or danger's moves instead.

Collectives

The collective size and its size factor are: One member (0), A handful of members (1), Several members (2), Many members (3), and A host of members (4).

When a single member of the collective is targeted, track its statuses separate from the collective. When a member in the collective is overcome, the MC decides whether to reduce the collective size by 1.

When the collective itself is the target of a status, reduce its incoming statuses by the size factor of the collective, then apply any other defenses it may have.

Optional Rules

Grit Mode: Grit mode scales the power used for moves, including the power used to create juice or apply tiers of a status. The scale is 1 = 1, 2-3 = 2, 4-6 = 3, and 7+ = 4. Negative power scales the same way.

Status Filter: The MC may allow you to set a status filter that decreases incoming statuses on a target, whether as a buff to decrease by negative conditions or a debuff to decreases positive conditions. To accomplish this, roll 10+ on CtG, choose the "Reduce or Remove a Status" option, and then spend another juice to prolong the effect of the created status. While the status filter is active, you cannot attack or defend the target with the tags used to set up the status filter.

Tag Cap: If players are often invoking four or more positive tags per move, the MC may cap the amount of tags allowed to be invoked per turn. A good cap is three. This may be used with grit mode.

Reversed Clues: The MC may allow you to spend a clue to introduce details into the investigation, and the MC will then add or adjust the details to fit the current case.

Grit Mode: Juice: The GM may choose to limit the CtG options for "burn a tag" and "reduce or remove a status" to temporary tags and statuses only. To affect ongoing story tags and statuses you must roll 10+ and spend a juice to make the effect ongoing.

Recover Burnt Tags with Juice: The MC may allow you to spend three juice to recover one power tag. Crispy power tags (eg. crew and extra power tags) may be recovered with just one juice.

Inescapable Sacrifice: The GM may add an additional degree of sacrifice to the SHB move, guaranteeing death or transformation.
    Inescapable: On 10+, you are killed, destroyed, or transformed forever (for now). Take a tier-6 status. You cannot Face Danger. On a 7-9, the same, and the MC chooses one: you only partly manage what you attempted OR your crew members, loved ones, or even the entire City is caught in the maelstrom of forces you unleashed.

Roll for Nemeses: The MC may choose to make the appearance of a nemesis random by having each player roll the following move during GODC.
    Foreshadow: At the end of the session, if your nemeses is still out there, roll+(the number of sessions since one of your Nemeses last appeared). On a 10+, one of your Nemeses will overwhelm you or get the drop on you in the next session. When the MC introduces your Nemesis, she also gets to make a move as if you missed a player move. On a 7-9, you can sense your Nemesis is coming but you’re not sure. The MC can choose whether to include your Nemesis in the next session or not. If she does, she does not get to make a surprise MC move. On a miss, you’re safe for now. Your Nemesis will not take part in the next sessio

Rebuild an Avatar: When you fall from Avatarhood, the MC may allow you to lose two of your three mythos themes but keep everything else, then perform a "Go Through a Transformation" Moment of Evolution with one mythos and three logos themes.

Rebuild a Sleeper: When you wake from your sleep, you may choose to lose two of your three logos themes but keep everything else, then perform a "go through a transformation" moment of evolution with one logos and three mythos themes.

Slow Theme Progression: If players mark attention too often, the MC may restrict the invoking of your own weakness tags to only once for each tag per scene. The MC and others may invoke those same weakness tags without restriction, which marks attention as usual.

Weakness Tags as Flaws: The MC may allow the invoking of your weaknesses outside of moves by either you or the MC. This marks attention on the theme, and then MC makes an MC move against you to describe how the flaw manifests in the fiction.
