After helping Finola get cleaned up, I ventured to Seoyun's room on the second floor, with the little faerie cradled in my hands since she had still not sobered. I knocked on the door and it opened a moment later.

Seoyun stood in the doorway, one arm braced against the frame like she were posing for a photo shoot. She greeted me with a vulpine smile and glittering amber eyes.

"Seth."

The sheer weight of the sensuality dripping from her voice resonated within me, and I instinctually felt the need to either throw myself at her or run for the hills. Instead, I held Finola closer to me and straightened my back.

"Hey, Seoyun. I need to head into town to pick up some snack supplies for our celebration, and I thought that if you wanted to stretch your legs some, I could really use your help," I suggested.

Seoyun tilted her head forward, her eyes never leaving mine, and her long wavy auburn hair flowed around her face to frame her features perfectly, like she had practiced the maneuver hundreds of times, which she may have.

"A road trip. Just you and I?" she practically purred, her fox ears twitching in excitement.

I cleared my throat, then nodded toward Finola. "And this little troublemaker."

Seoyun's eyes narrowed and she pulled back, crinkling her nose as though I were holding a bundle of sweaty gym socks rather than an adorable but intoxicated little girl. "Bah. Finola? I'll have to think about it."

"Please?"

Obviously I didn't want anyone hurt, but I also couldn't deny a guest sustinence, and furthermore, anything that happened outside the grounds of the boardinghouse was technically no longer our responsibility.

"Yeah." I sighed, dropping my shoulders a fraction. "Yeah, okay. Just be careful."

Seoyun darted forward and planted a kiss on my cheek before I could respond.

"Thanks, sweetie," she crooned, then quickly disappeared down the hallway toward the stairs, two large and fluffy fox tails trailing behind her.

From my hands, I heard Finola singing, "Seoyun and Seth, sitting in a tree. K-I-S-S-I-N-G."

Moments later, the kumiho and I got into my sporty hatchback and headed into town with Finola resting in the back seat.

The dirt road leading into the town of Forest Glen was long and winding, framed by thick barriers of trees on both sides. The surrounding forest went on as far as the eye could see, rising along the hills that encircled the town, and gave the distinct feeling that the town and the boardinghouse were cut off from the rest of the world.

Night was fully upon us and the rain was falling in sheets. The downpour blocked out the moonlight and turned the dirt road into muddy slop. It was probably insane for me to drive in these conditions, but I was determined to make this anniversary celebration a success, and for that we needed party supplies.

Seoyun laughed merrily. "Oh, I have no doubt."

She grasped my knee to punctuate her statement, startling me. The timing couldn't have been worse, and I oversteered as I entered the next turn. Seoyun laughed in delight, and her hand squeezed my knee as I worked the accelerator and clutch, steering into the slide to right the vehicle. My heart was pounding in my chest and my knuckles were white from my death grip on the steering wheel, but we were now facing the proper direction as we zoomed down the treacherous road through the blinding rain.

"Dammit," I grumbled under my breath.

I could sense Seoyun grinning at me out of the corner of my eye. "You almost killed us!" She sounded thrilled.

We both knew that I was the only one of the three in the car that was at risk of going out in an embarrassing vehicular death, but I didn't feel the need to correct her or fuel her teasing.

When I didn't respond, the kumiho chuckled softly and ruffled my hair. "You're no fun. Just relax and enjoy the drive." She withdrew her hand and gazed out her window. "The rain is really soothing."

Seoyun wasn't wrong, and while the driving conditions never improved, eventually the quiet in the car and the sounds of the rain beating on the roof lulled me into a state of calm. The remainder of our trip into town was blessedly uneventful.
