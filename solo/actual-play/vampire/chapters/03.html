The group returned to their base below the Christmas shoppe and congregated in the rest area with the couch and boiler. Vincent stood guard just outside the room while Charlotte collapses lazily on the couch across from the boiler. Corinna paced angrily back and forth, consumed in thought.

Katherine watched Corinna with a worried expression. "Am I...allowed to go now? I did what you needed, didn't I?"

Corinna stopped pacing. "Oh no. We have more uses for you, girl."

"What? But I thought--" Katherine froze mid-sentence when Corinna appeared before her in the time it took for her to blink.

"Your blood is special. Very special," Corinna said as she lightly traced a line down Katherine's neck with her fingernail. "As Vanessa's descendant, you are the antithesis to her powers. Her blood magic, her compulsion; all of it can be negated with your blood."

"Sorry, Kat," Charlotte said from the couch.

Margaret shook her head. "Why do you even care about Vanessa? You're free now. Just let it go."

"You!" Corinna spun toward Margaret. "This is all because of you!" She slammed Margaret against the wall and held her there with a hand against her chest. "Are you ready to die?"

Margaret glared at her. "Go ahead."

Corinna reared back her other hand. Her nails glinted in the light like knives. "Why did you do it?" she asked.

Margaret never broke her stare but didn't say a word. The onlookers were frozen, unsure whether they had the right or the desire to get involved.

"Nothing to say for yourself, human?" Corinna growled.

Margaret looked away. "That's right. I'm just a worthless human. Just a plaything for vampires whenever it suits your whimsy and tossed aside when it doesn't."

"What are prattling on about, woman?" the small vampire asked.

Margaret snapped her head back toward Corinna. "You don't remember tossing me out like trash?"

Margaret glared like she could bore a hole through Corinna with her eyes. When Corinna didn't respond, she continued, "You and Charlotte were all that I had left as a child after my family died from disease. You gave me a home and we became a family. But that wasn't enough, was it? You needed to be a queen with your own coven. A coven that no longer needed a human."

"You're a grown woman. You can manage," Charlotte said from the couch.

"You were my family!" Margaret shouted at her. "My home was with you... and you took that away from me."

"Then you feel justified in having sealed me away for decades?" Corinna asked, her voice full of anger.

"That wasn't supposed to happen. Vanessa... she double crossed me. Your confinement was the concession that she made after much bargaining. She wanted you dead."

"And what did you want? Revenge?" Charlotte asked.

"No!" Margaret shut her eyes tight. "No, I just wanted my family back. Vanessa promised to disband your coven. And she did. She removed Corinna as the head and stole the coven right out from under her." She sighed in frustration. "But what good was that? She captured Corinna and marked me as a traitor to you all. Along with disbanding your coven, she disbanded my family."

The room was quiet with no one quite sure what to say. Corinna released Margaret but remained standing before her.

"Ive been trying to free you," Margaret said looking at Corinna. "The binding spell was very specific. It was tied to her blood. No one could break the spell without it, or so I thought," she quickly glanced at Katherine, "but God did I try. I would cast blood magic until I thought I was going to pass out from anemia."

"Hmmph," was all Corinna said in response. She crossed her arms and looked up at the tall red-head. After a long while she said, "You may stay for now."

Charlotte sat up straight. "Are you sure? I don't tru--"

"I said she stays, Charlotte," Corinna snapped. Charlotte clamped her mouth shut like a chastised child.

"Is this my life now? Am I to become this woman?" Katherine asks, gesturing toward Margaret.

"Hold your tongue, girl," Margaret shot back.

"Your life is your own, Katherine," Vincent said from the doorway.

"Is it though? You alone dragged me from my home, as miserable as it was, and now I'm held as a prisoner here for whatever this little girl's plans are."

Corinna bristled. "I told you that I'm not a little girl."

Katherine dismissed her with a wave of her hand. Corinna scoffed.

"I vowed that I would protect you. I intend to keep that promise," Vincent said.

Charlotte stood up. "Protect her? She's just a blood bag!"

"Don't be jealous, Charlotte. It's unseemly," Margaret said with a smirk.

Corinna nodded. "This is good. Vincent shall be her bodyguard."

Charlotte gasped. "But Corinna..!"

Corinna rolled her eyes. "Margaret was right. This is unbecoming. Control yourself."

Charlotte pouted.

Margaret stroked her chin in thought. "This is strange, however. There was a prophecy once presented to Vanessa. In it, her kine was to be the death of her. We didn't know what that meant at the time, but..." She turned to Katherine. "How did your parents die?"

"In a fire..," She responded.

"I see. Yes, that is classic Vanessa. I have no doubt that she knew of your family. I don't believe that she intended for you to survive."

"I nearly didn't!" Katherine said wide-eyed. "But you're saying that she is the one that killed my family? She destroyed my life?!"

Margaret nodded.

"That bitch!" Katherine turned to Corinna with a look of rage. "I will help you. She has to die."

Corinna clapped her hands. "Excellent!"

Margaret holds up a hand. "There was more to the prophecy than just Vanessa's death. The kine will die as well."

Corinna laughed. "Hardly. Despite her age, Vanessa would be no match for you or Charlotte. No, what makes Vanessa dangerous is her guile. She's a cunning temptress who speaks with eloquence and knows exactly what you want to hear." She gave a sideways glance at Margaret who lowered her head in disgrace. "She has amassed an army with this ability."

"What's the plan then?" Charlotte asked. She was now sitting backwards atop the couch to face the conversation taking place behind it.

Corinna turned to Margaret. "You know her best. Would it be more prudent to attack her head on or wait for her strike?"

Last time she wasn't expecting you to free Corinna or else she would have left her guarded. This time she'll be expecting you and will likely have increased defenses around the castle."

"So, then...," Vincent prodded.

"So then we wait. Here. Vanessa knows that Katherine is a threat, especially now that she was able to nullify her binding spell on Corinna. She will come for her. Trust me."

Katherine shrugged and sighed. "It looks like I'm to be bait as well as a blood bag."

Margaret gave her a sympathetic smile.

"Yes, it appears so." Corinna said. Ignoring Katherine's plight, she instead analyzed her surroundings. "This place will serve adequately as a stronghold. You have done well, Charlotte."

Charlotte blinked. "Oh. Uh, thank you," she said, clearly taken off guard by the compliment regarding the housing that she found at random in a moment of desperation.

Corinna leaned against the wall. "Now we wait for them to come to us."
