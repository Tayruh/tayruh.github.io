Chaos Rank: 5
Threads: None
NPCs: None

Sarah pulls up to the school in her bright red Supra. She gets some looks, which she finds annoying. Perhaps driving a flashy car isn't the best way to stay out of the spot light.

She leaves her car in the lot and heads toward the school.

Q: Does someone stop her along the way? (Likely)
A: Yes

Complex Q: Who is it?
A: Partially Scary

As Sarah walks up the steps to the school, she hears a set of footsteps moving in time with her own. She turns her head toward the sound and sees her friend April. April beams at her. Her angelic face sends a chill through Sarah.

"April," she says under her breath, "what are you doing here?"

Complex Q: What's her motive?
A: Attainment Benefits

"It's your first day of school, right? I want to enjoy it with you."

Sarah looks around to make sure no one is watching her. "April, you know it's my first day. I haven't been back to school since… Since I went away. After… you know…"

April giggles and bounds off into the school, completely ignoring Sarah's plight.

Sarah just sighs and follows her friend inside.

Complex Q: What happens when she enters?
A: Block Lies

Complex Q: Who?
A: Hopelessy Lonely

"Who were you talking to?"

"Wha..?" Sarah blurts out, having been caught off guard by a voice that seemed to come out of nowhere. It apparently belonged the short blond haired girl standing just to the side of the door.

"Who were you talking to?" she repeats politely.

"… Myself? I was just thinking out loud."

I could have almost earned a plot point here, but the girl made Sarah lie about April, not the past.

"Mmmm." She puts a finger to her lips, unconvinced. Changing the subject, she asks "Are you new here?"

"Yeah. It's my first day."

"Kind of late into the year, don't you think?"

"Yeah, well, there were… circumstances."

"Oooh. Do tell."

"I'd rather not. I should be going anyway."

The girl lets out a laugh like a fallen angel. "Oh, I pushed a button. How fun."

Sarah just glares at her for a few seconds before she begins to walk away. The girl reaches out for her. "Stop. Do you even know where you're going?"

"The principal's office."

"That's not even in the right direction. Let me guide you."

Sarah gives her a skeptical look. "Why?"

"Why not? I'm bored. Let's go."

As Sarah leaves the classroom she almost immediately crashes into a figure. The girl that she ran into glares up at her before realizing who it was.

"Oh! It's the new girl!" says the girl that she met earlier. She takes a step back and smoothes out her clothes. Behind her stands a tall red head, who raises an eyebrow as she appraises Sarah. "Kristen, this is Sarah," the short blonde says over her shoulder to the redhead, "the one that I told you about."

"The weird one?" she asks, still looking Sarah up and down.

The girl lets out a laugh. "Yeah, the weird one." She turns to Sarah, "I'm Chelsea, by the way. I never introduced myself."

Sarah just nods. Her eyes looking for an exit. "Look, I've got to go. It was good seeing you again, Chelsea. Kristen."

Chelsea tries to persuade Sarah to come with them using Forever Alone D8 distinction, Sarah D6 relationship, Social D10 attribute, and Influence D10 skill. She gets a 19 with a D8 effect die.

Sarah contests with Social D6 attribute, Chelsea D6 relationship, and Influence D8 skill. Chelsea is definitely much more in her natural habit than Sarah. She gets a 6 with a D8 effect. She's absolutely taken out.

The difference of 13 bumps Chelsea's effect die up to D12. Chelsea gives Sarah the D12 complication Chelsea's Pet Project.

She tries to squeeze past Chelsea immediately links her arm with Sarah's.

"Nope!" Chelsea says with a grin. "You're coming with us."

"Yeah, but—"

"Shhh! No backtalk," Chelsea says and pulls her down the hall. Kristen just shakes her head and smiles.

I've tried both stream of consciousness writing and bullet points, and while bullet points do leave out a lot of nuance, they get the job done faster and easier. Not only do they allow you to quickly continue with the story before you lose your motivation, but its abstraction alleviates the concern of trying to write well. You can also add finer details to bullet points—like a specific quote from a character—whenever you feel it's necessary.
