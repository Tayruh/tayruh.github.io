Charlotte and company arrived at the castle just after nightfall. Rather than approaching the gate, the group traversed to the darkest corner of the castle wall. The castle walls may keep out humans but they were nothing to vampires.

Katherine latched onto Vincent's back with her arms wrapped tightly around his shoulders. Charlotte gave side-eyed looks from apparent jealousy, but there wasn't any other way to get the human over the wall.

The cobblestone walls of the castle provided convenient handholds and Vincent and Charlotte quickly ascended to the top of the wall.

The trio descended the near stairs and entered the castle.

As if to answer her concerns, the next turn led them straight into a command room, not unlike her own. In it was a desk and behind it stood Margaret.

The red-haired woman appeared to have been glancing over some documents on their arrival, but her head snapped up to spot them the moment that they appeared.

"Why, if it isn't my lovely Vincent. How are you, my sweet?" The voluptuous woman gracefully skirted around the desk and latched onto his arm. Margaret beamed at him but then her smile withered into a grimace once she glanced over his shoulder. "Oh. I see your keeper tagged along as well."

"Hello, Margaret," Charlotte said flatly and unamused.

Margaret ignored Charlotte and returned her attention to Vincent. "I haven't seen you in ages. What brings you to my humble lair?"

"We came to retrieve something of ours," Vincent said matter-of-factly. Charlotte gasped at how he showed their hand.

Margaret rubbed her cheek on Vincent's shoulder like a kitten. "Something? Or someone?" she asked, peering up at him to search his face.

When he didn't answer she just sighed. "Why her? Corinna has been ours for decades and you only just now come to her rescue. What changed?"

"If you must know," Charlotte said as she stepped forward and gave Margaret a light shove to separate her from Vincent, "we are leaving this city and we're not leaving her behind."

Margaret giggled and let Charlotte push her away, happy to have gotten the reaction that she was looking for.

"Do you know where she is?" Vincent asked Margaret.

"Hmmm.. I don't know. Do I?" the red-head feigned ignorance.

"Margaret. Please," Vincent said.

"What do you mean 'show us'?" Charlotte asked.

"I will guide you." Margaret glomped onto Vincent's arm. "After all, I can't let my Vinciepoo fall into a trap, can I? I would worry so!"

Charlotte narrowed her eyes. "Lead the way, tramp."

Margaret cackled as she practically dragged Vincent out of the room by his elbow.

The group traveled through the castle, following Margaret's lead. Charlotte was loath to admit it, but having Margaret declare their path was crucial, since she not only knew the exact location Corinna, but also the location of all guard patrols. They didn't meet a soul on their way to where Corinna was sealed.

"Well, this is drab," Charlotte said upon arriving as their destination.

The room was a dark holding cell. There was absolutely nothing to see other than an elaborately decorated coffin in the center of the room.

"A coffin? I thought those were just fiction," Katherine said.

"They are fiction. Vanessa thinks that she's being cute," Charlotte said while grimacing at Margaret. Margaret just smiled and shrugged.

Vincent stepped toward the box. "Right. So, how do we do this?"

Q: How are they going to do this?

"Well, we need Katherine, first of all," Charlotte began, "and then we need a touch of magic. I was going to attempt the act myself since Corinna has taught me a little bit of sorcerery, buuut...," she turned to Margaret, "we have had the fortune of being graced with our very one blood mage. Haven't we, dear?"

Margaret laughed. "Oh, please. You know that I have the skill, but it's going to take more than the blood of a virgin to wake your precious Corinna."

Q: How is Katherine's blood special?

"I know," Charlotte said.

"I don't think you do. This wasn't just any spell that put her under; the spell was tied to Vanessa's blood. You can't break the seal without it."

"That's incorrect. It was tied to her bloodline, not her blood."

"Sure, but Vanessa is a vampire. She doesn't have descendants."

Charlotte grinned like the cat that got the cream. "Wrong again!"

Margaret was quiet as she took this in.

"Vanessa had a child?" she finally asked.

Charlotte shrugged. "Seems like." Then she waved her arm in the directly of Katherine. "Allow me to introduce to you the last living descendant of Vanessa."

Margaret turned her attention the young blonde and looked her up and down.

"Excuse me, but who is Vanessa?" Katherine asked, shrinking back from Margaret's scrutinizing gaze.

"She's our princess," Margaret answered. "She rules our coven."

Charlotte laughed. "Your coven? You leech."

"Whatever," Margaret waved a hand in dismissal. "They provide room and board in exchange for my services. I may not be a vampire, but this is the closest thing I've had to a home since... well.. you know."

Charlotte glared. "Yes, I know."

Vincent coughed. "Right then. Can we do this now?"

Margaret sighed. "Yeah, fine." She turned to Katherine. "Girl, hold your wrist out over the coffin."

Katherine gulped but complied. She held her delicate wrist directly above a golden rose emblazoned on the lid of the coffin. Margaret held Katherine's wrist with in hand and broke her pale skin with the thumbnail of her other. Katherine hissed at the sharp pain.

Margaret whisped words in an ancient language as blood trickled from Katherine's wrist and pooled on the rose. It traveled along the grooves of the intricate engraving. When the petals had been outlined by Katherine's blood, the blood glowed softly and the lid popped with a gentle click.

"Wow... It's true," Margaret said as she looked at Katherine once more.

Katherine took her arm back and held her wrist, putting pressure on the wound.

Q: Does Corinna wake immediately? (Yes/And, Straight, Drama)

With the sound of splintering wood, a small fist thrust up through the lid of the coffin, snapped it in two, and blew it off its hinges. The coffin was vacated faster than even Charlotte could see and that small hand held Margaret against the wall by her throat. Margaret's feet kicked as she dangled and grasped at her throat, trying to pry Corinna's steel grip from her trachea.

"Betrayer!" Corinna shouted with a guttural growl. The petite vampire radiated power and fury. Magically charged currents circled her, whipping her long black hair, through which peered eyes that glittered gold in the light.

"Cor--" was all Margaret could get out before Corinna squeezed tighter and cut off any ability to speak.

"I should kill you right now," Corinna hissed.

Vincent spoke up. "Corinna. Margaret is the one that freed you."

Corinna whipped her head toward Vincent and glared intensely. Without looking, she pushed Margaret harder against the wall, causing her to whimper. Corinna then turned to Charlotte. "Is this true?"

Charlotte shrugged and sighed. "Yeah?"

Corinna returned her gaze to Margaret. The red-head's struggling was becoming slow and clumsy. She looked like she was about to pass out. Corinna observed her, still seething with rage. Then as Margaret's eyes were beginning to roll back into her head, Corinna opened her palm wide and let the woman fall to the ground in a heap.

Katherine had hid behind Vincent and watched the violent display. Unconsciously she whispered to herself, "She's just a little girl..."

"Who's a little girl?" Corinna's voice came from just behind her shoulder.

Katherine froze.

"Whats the matter?" the voice of the child whispered into her ear, so close that her breath tickled. "Are you frightened of a little girl?"

Katherine remained silent and motionless.

Corinna sniffed her. "You smell amazing. Do you know that?" she asked. Katherine only whimpered in reply.

"Corinna, dear. You don't want that one," Charlotte said.

Corinna frowned. "Why not?"

"This is Vanessa's descendant."

"Ha..!" A laugh caught in Corinna's throat. Then she laughed like a mad woman. Her laughter echoed off the walls.

Unsurprisingly, a pair of patrolling guards heard her laughter down the hall and ran into the room to check the disturbance. One of the men spotted the broken coffin and immediately darted out of the room and around the corner. Seconds later the sound of something like a church bell rang out.

Corinna chuckled to herself and wiped tears of laughter from her eyes. Charlotte shook her shoulders. "Cor, we need to go. Now."

The small vampire looked over her shoulder at her kindred, then at the guards piling into the room. "Yes. I suppose it's that time."
