The changes are as follows:

I wasn't sure whether that meant he'd be her pranking victim or accomplice, but Seoyun and I left her to it.

Moments later, the two of us arrived at the door to kitchen at long last. We stepped inside and were greeted with the sight of my dear sister bustling away in an animated fashion. Erin was in the process of assembling a large snack platter on the island in the middle of the kitchen. Her hair was a mess, like she had just run a marathon. You would think by how frazzled she looked that we were hosting a party for a hundred guests, not just the six tenants that lived with us.

I tried not to disrupt her workspace as I placed my bags on the far end of the island. Erin looked up, and seeing me standing there, she smiled wide.

"Great. You're back." She blew an annoying strand of hair out of her face. "Any trouble getting the stuff?"

Seoyun placed her bag next to mine. "Only that boogerman asshat getting in our faces, like usual."

"Ugh. I hate that guy." Erin's shoulders drooped and she sighed. "I actually really love this job, except for him."

Seoyun wrinkled her brow. "He harasses you because you and Seth are the only humans around, and Seth is nearly impossible to scare."

She actually sounded like she was concerned for Erin. That seemed odd to me. If she really did consider humans to just be food, she wouldn't have had any qualms about Gray feeding off of Erin. I wondered whether the kumiho cared for Erin as more than just a friendly rival and emergency food. And if that were the case, how did she actually feel about Melody?

Going with that line of thought, I asked Erin, "What do you think about maybe having Melody come visit?"

I was stunned. I'm not sure that it occurred to anyone that Jess would assume we were intending to replace her in anything, least of all as Erin's friend. I had honestly expected the three of them to become a trio of friends. I was beginning to suspect that Jess was incapable of sharing anyone.

My thoughts were interrupted by Jess leaping at me like a damned cat. Except this cat could rend steel with her claws, and I was far less less durable than that.

Jess pulled herself from the ruined woodwork, and clung to the intact portions of the cupboards with her hands and feet laid flat against it.
